I use interdisciplinary research methods and programming as a playground to create and discover new mannerisms through the intersection of design and technology. Currently, I am a Design MFA Candidate at University of California, Davis. I am working as a teaching assistant. Secret gardens, hobbit houses and filled up journals excite me. Peculiar behaviors, surrealism, and wild technological ecology

inspire me. I have shown work in San Francisco at the Exploratorium, Gray Area Foundation for the Arts, Jan Shrem and Maria Manetti Shrem Museum of Art and The Mondavi Preforming Arts Center. Going forward, I am writing, researching and mapping my thesis. My research topics include, the quantified self, perceptual psycology, and data visualization.
